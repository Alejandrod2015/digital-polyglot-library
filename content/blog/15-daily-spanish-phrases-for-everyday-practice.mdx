Learning a new language, like Spanish, can seem overwhelming at first, but a practical and consistent approach is the key to success. Incorporating useful, everyday phrases into your routine not only improves your vocabulary but also helps you gain confidence when speaking. Below, we present 15 daily Spanish phrases you can practice, divided into categories to make learning easier. Let’s begin!

### Daily Spanish Phrases for Greetings and Farewells
Mastering greetings and farewells is crucial for building confidence in any language. These essential daily Spanish phrases will help you start and end conversations smoothly in both formal and casual settings.

**1.Hola, ¿cómo estás?  
**_Translation:_ Hello, how are you?  
It’s one of the most basic and friendly ways to start a conversation. Use it when meeting friends, family, or even new people.

**2.Buenos días / Buenas tardes / Buenas noches  
**_Translation:_ Good morning / Good afternoon / Good evening  
These are specific ways to greet someone depending on the time of day. “Buenos días” is for the morning, “buenas tardes” for the afternoon, and “buenas noches” once the sun goes down.

**3.Adiós / Hasta luego  
**_Translation:_ Goodbye / See you later  
To say goodbye, “adiós” is more formal, but “hasta luego” is more common in casual settings and means “see you later.”

### Polite Phrases
Politeness can go a long way in making a good impression. These polite and useful daily Spanish phrases will help you express gratitude, request something, or respond kindly, ensuring your interactions are both friendly and respectful.

**4.[Por favor](https://dle.rae.es/favor?m=form#6uaIcnP)  
**_Translation:_ Please  
One of the most important words in any language. Use it when making a request, such as: “Can you pass me the water, please?”

**5.Gracias / Muchas gracias  
**_Translation:_ Thank you / Thank you very much  
Don’t forget to express gratitude. “Gracias” is universally understood and appreciated. You can use “muchas gracias” to show even more appreciation.

**6.De nada / No hay de qué  
**_Translation:_ You’re welcome / No problem  
When someone thanks you, these are polite responses. Both phrases mean essentially the same thing.

### Asking for Information
Whether you’re traveling or just exploring your city, knowing how to ask for help or information in Spanish is vital. These key daily Spanish phrases will make sure you can navigate through new environments with ease, asking for directions, prices, or assistance.

**7\. ¿Dónde está…?  
**_Translation:_ Where is…?  
This phrase is essential if you need to find places like a bathroom, a store, or a train station. For example: “¿Dónde está el baño?” (Where is the bathroom?).

**8\. ¿Cuánto cuesta?  
**_Translation:_ How much does it cost?  
Useful in any shopping situation, this phrase lets you ask for the price of a product or service.

**9\. ¿Puedes ayudarme?  
**_Translation:_ Can you help me?  
If you’re lost or need assistance, this is the perfect phrase to politely ask for help.

### Daily Use Phrases
Incorporating commonly used daily Spanish phrases into your routine is the best way to improve fluency. These phrases cover basic daily needs, like expressing hunger, asking for the time, or managing everyday conversations.

**10\. ¿Qué hora es?  
**_Translation:_ What time is it?  
Useful in almost any situation, whether coordinating plans or simply checking the time.

**11\. Tengo hambre / Tengo sed  
**_Translation:_ I’m hungry / I’m thirsty  
These phrases are perfect for expressing your basic needs when you’re out or at a friend’s house.

**12\. Está bien / No importa  
**_Translation:_ It’s okay / It doesn’t matter  
If something doesn’t go as planned or if someone apologizes, these phrases help you stay calm and show understanding.

### Basic Conversation Phrases
Sometimes, the simplest phrases are the most important. These basic but essential daily Spanish phrases will help you keep conversations going, ask for clarification, or express when you don’t understand something. Perfect for learners at any level.

![](/blog/15-daily-spanish-phrases-for-everyday-practice/image-39.webp)

**13\. Lo siento  
**_Translation:_ I’m sorry  
Use it to apologize, whether for small mistakes or bigger misunderstandings. It’s a very versatile and useful phrase.

**14\. ¿Puedes repetir, por favor?  
**_Translation:_ Can you repeat, please?  
In a conversation, if you don’t understand something, this phrase allows you to politely ask the other person to repeat what they said.

**15\. No entiendo / No sé  
**_Translation:_ I don’t understand / I don’t know  
You won’t always know all the answers or words, and that’s okay. These phrases help you navigate situations where you need clarification or simply don’t know the answer.

### How to Practice These Phrases Daily

-   **Use a phrase notebook:** Carry a small notebook or use a [note-taking app](https://www.notion.so) on your phone to jot down these phrases and any new ones you learn. Keeping track of your progress will help reinforce your memory.
-   **Talk to yourself:** Repeat the phrases in front of a mirror or while doing daily activities. This will help you memorize them and improve your fluency. You can also use [language learning platforms](https://www.duolingo.com) that offer speaking exercises to further enhance your practice.
