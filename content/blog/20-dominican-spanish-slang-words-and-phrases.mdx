Dominican Spanish is quick, musical, and famous for swallowing letters and reinventing words on the spot. **Dominican Spanish slang** carries the island's humor and its street rhythm, and a few of these words will get you smiling with locals in Santo Domingo or Santiago in no time.

Below are 20 **Dominican Spanish slang words and phrases** you will hear everywhere. For each one you get the meaning, an example in context, and the English translation, so you know exactly how and when to use it.

## Dominican Slang for Greetings and People

This is how Dominicans say hello and talk about the people around them.

### 1. Qué lo que

**Meaning**: What's up? The classic Dominican greeting, often written *klk* in texts.

**Example**: "¡Qué lo que, manín!" ("What's up, bro!")

### 2. Manín

**Meaning**: Bro, buddy, mate. An affectionate word for a friend.

**Example**: "Dime, manín, ¿cómo va todo?" ("Tell me, bro, how's everything going?")

### 3. Tíguere

**Meaning**: A sharp, streetwise guy who always finds a way. It can be admiring or a warning, depending on tone.

**Example**: "Ese tíguere se las sabe todas." ("That guy knows every trick in the book.")

### 4. Fren

**Meaning**: Friend, taken straight from English. Very common among younger Dominicans.

**Example**: "Vino con sus frenes." ("He came with his friends.")

### 5. Pariguayo

**Meaning**: A fool, a pushover, or someone who just stands around doing nothing.

**Example**: "No seas pariguayo, di algo." ("Don't be a wallflower, say something.")

## Dominican Slang for Good Times and Messes

Dominicans have a word for everything from all good to total chaos.

### 6. Tato

**Meaning**: All good, okay, sorted. A shortening of *está todo (bien)*.

**Example**: "¿Nos vemos mañana? Tato." ("See you tomorrow? All good.")

### 7. Bacano

**Meaning**: Cool, awesome, great. High praise for a person, plan, or thing.

**Example**: "Ese lugar está bacano." ("That place is awesome.")

### 8. Se formó

**Meaning**: It kicked off. Used for a party getting good or for trouble breaking out.

**Example**: "Llegó la música y se formó." ("The music came on and it kicked off.")

### 9. Chercha

**Meaning**: Joking around, banter, a fun time full of laughs.

**Example**: "Estábamos en chercha toda la noche." ("We were joking around all night.")

### 10. Vaina seria

**Meaning**: A heavy or serious matter. It can be good news or bad, so tone tells you which.

**Example**: "Eso que pasó fue una vaina seria." ("What happened was a serious thing.")

## Dominican Slang for Everyday Life

The words that fill ordinary Dominican conversation.

### 11. Vaina

**Meaning**: Thing, stuff, whatsit. The most Dominican word of all, used for literally anything.

**Example**: "Pásame esa vaina." ("Hand me that thing.")

### 12. Chin

**Meaning**: A little bit, a touch. *Un chin* is a small amount of anything.

**Example**: "Dame un chin de café." ("Give me a little bit of coffee.")

### 13. Concho

**Meaning**: A shared route taxi. A *motoconcho* is the motorbike version, everywhere on the island.

**Example**: "Cogí un concho hasta el centro." ("I took a shared taxi to downtown.")

### 14. Colmado

**Meaning**: The corner store, and the social heart of any Dominican neighborhood.

**Example**: "Voy al colmado a comprar algo." ("I'm going to the corner store to buy something.")

### 15. Jumo

**Meaning**: Drunkenness. *Tener un jumo* is to be drunk.

**Example**: "Anoche cogió un jumo tremendo." ("Last night he got really drunk.")

### 16. Coro

**Meaning**: A party, a hangout, a good time with friends.

**Example**: "Esta noche hay coro en casa de Juan." ("Tonight there's a party at Juan's place.")

### 17. Ñapa

**Meaning**: A little something extra, thrown in for free. *De ñapa* means on top of everything else.

**Example**: "Me dio una ñapa en el colmado." ("He gave me a little extra at the store.")

### 18. Deguabinao

**Meaning**: Worn out, drained, exhausted. You will also hear *desguabinao*.

**Example**: "Terminé el día deguabinao." ("I ended the day completely worn out.")

### 19. Bulto

**Meaning**: A bluff, empty talk, all show and no substance. *Eso es bulto* means that's just talk.

**Example**: "No le hagas caso, eso es puro bulto." ("Don't mind him, that's all talk.")

### 20. Dar una bola

**Meaning**: To give someone a ride. You will also hear *dar un pon*.

**Example**: "¿Me das una bola hasta la casa?" ("Can you give me a ride home?")
