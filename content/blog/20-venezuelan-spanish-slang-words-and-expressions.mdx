Venezuelan Spanish is loud, warm, and impossible to confuse with any other accent in Latin America. **Venezuelan Spanish slang** carries the country's humor, its love of exaggeration, and a talent for turning a simple hello into a whole conversation. Learn a handful of these words and any chat in Caracas, Maracaibo, or Valencia will warm up instantly.

Below are 20 **Venezuelan Spanish slang words and expressions** locals really use. For each one you get the meaning, an example in context, and the English translation, so you know exactly how and when to say it.

## Venezuelan Slang for People and Greetings

This is how Venezuelans call out to each other and get a conversation going.

### 1. Épale

**Meaning**: Hey, hi. The default Venezuelan greeting, friendly and casual. You will also hear *épa*.

**Example**: "¡Épale, chamo! ¿Cómo estás?" ("Hey, dude! How are you?")

### 2. Chamo / chama

**Meaning**: Guy, girl, dude. The single most Venezuelan word for a young person or a friend.

**Example**: "Ese chamo es amigo mío." ("That guy is a friend of mine.")

### 3. Pana

**Meaning**: Close friend, buddy. Being *pana* with someone means you are tight.

**Example**: "Tranquilo, pana, yo te ayudo." ("Relax, buddy, I'll help you.")

### 4. Musiú

**Meaning**: A foreigner, usually a European or North American. It comes from the French *monsieur*.

**Example**: "El musiú habla buen español." ("The foreigner speaks good Spanish.")

### 5. Jeva / jevo

**Meaning**: Girlfriend or boyfriend, or just a girl or guy you fancy.

**Example**: "Salió con su jeva." ("He went out with his girlfriend.")

## Venezuelan Slang for Reactions

Venezuelans react out loud, and these are the words they reach for.

### 6. Naguará

**Meaning**: Wow. A cry of surprise, admiration, or shock, especially loved in the Lara region.

**Example**: "¡Naguará, qué grande esa casa!" ("Wow, that house is huge!")

### 7. Conchale

**Meaning**: Aw man, come on. Mild dismay or disappointment, often stretched into *conchale, vale*.

**Example**: "Conchale, vale, se dañó el carro otra vez." ("Aw man, the car broke down again.")

### 8. Chévere

**Meaning**: Cool, great, nice. Heard across the region but deeply Venezuelan in spirit.

**Example**: "Esa película está chévere." ("That movie is great.")

### 9. Fino

**Meaning**: Cool, fine, all good. A quick way to say something is great or to agree.

**Example**: "¿Nos vemos a las ocho? Fino." ("See you at eight? Cool.")

### 10. Arrecho

**Meaning**: A do it all word. It can mean awesome and impressive, or angry, depending on tone and context.

**Example**: "Ese concierto estuvo arrecho." ("That concert was awesome.") / "Está arrecho porque llegaron tarde." ("He's angry because they arrived late.")

### 11. Vale

**Meaning**: Man, dude, or okay. A filler tacked onto sentences, and also plain agreement.

**Example**: "Vamos ya, vale." ("Let's go now, man.")

## Venezuelan Slang for Everyday Life

The words that color ordinary conversation.

### 12. Burda (de)

**Meaning**: A lot, very, tons. *Burda de bueno* means really good.

**Example**: "Me gusta burda esa canción." ("I like that song a lot.")

### 13. Ladilla

**Meaning**: A nuisance, a pain, or an annoying person or task. *Qué ladilla* means how annoying.

**Example**: "Ese trámite es una ladilla." ("That paperwork is such a pain.")

### 14. Bochinche

**Meaning**: A commotion, a loud mess, or gossip and drama.

**Example**: "Se formó tremendo bochinche en la fiesta." ("A big commotion broke out at the party.")

### 15. Chimbo / chimba

**Meaning**: Fake, low quality, or bad. The opposite of *fino*.

**Example**: "Ese teléfono salió chimbo." ("That phone turned out to be junk.")

### 16. Coroto

**Meaning**: A thing, a thingamajig. *Los corotos* are your belongings or stuff.

**Example**: "Recoge tus corotos, que nos vamos." ("Grab your stuff, we're leaving.")

### 17. Ratón

**Meaning**: A hangover. *Tener ratón* is to be hungover the morning after.

**Example**: "Amanecí con un ratón horrible." ("I woke up with a terrible hangover.")

### 18. Rumba

**Meaning**: A party or a night out. *Irse de rumba* is to go out partying.

**Example**: "Anoche nos fuimos de rumba." ("Last night we went out partying.")

### 19. Cuaima

**Meaning**: A very jealous, controlling partner. A word Venezuelans use with a knowing smile.

**Example**: "Su novia es una cuaima." ("His girlfriend is super jealous.")

### 20. Echar los perros

**Meaning**: To flirt with someone, to hit on them, to make a move.

**Example**: "Le está echando los perros a la vecina." ("He's flirting with the neighbor.")
