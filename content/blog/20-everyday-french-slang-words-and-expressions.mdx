Textbook French will get you through a bakery, but real conversations in France run on **French slang words**, known as argot. This is the register friends use over a coffee, in text messages, and on the metro, and it is the fastest way to stop sounding like a walking grammar exercise.

Below are 20 everyday **French slang words and expressions** locals really use. For each one you get the meaning, an example in context, and the English translation, so you know exactly how and when to say it.

## French Slang for Greetings and People

Start here, because this is who you are talking to and how you get their attention.

### 1. Wesh

**Meaning**: Hey, what's up. A very casual greeting, common among younger speakers.

**Example**: "Wesh, ça va ou quoi?" ("Hey, you good or what?")

### 2. Mec

**Meaning**: Guy, dude, bloke. Everywhere in spoken French.

**Example**: "C'est un mec sympa." ("He's a nice guy.")

### 3. Meuf

**Meaning**: Woman, girl, or girlfriend. It is *femme* flipped into backslang, which the French call verlan.

**Example**: "Sa meuf est trop cool." ("His girlfriend is really cool.")

### 4. Un pote

**Meaning**: A mate, a buddy. *Mon pote* is my pal.

**Example**: "Je sors avec mes potes ce soir." ("I'm going out with my mates tonight.")

### 5. Frangin / frangine

**Meaning**: Brother and sister, used affectionately for real siblings or close friends.

**Example**: "Je te présente mon frangin." ("Let me introduce my brother.")

## Verlan: French Slang Flipped Backwards

Verlan reverses the syllables of a word, and it is everywhere in France. The name itself is *l'envers* (the reverse) flipped around. Here are four you will hear constantly.

### 6. Ouf

**Meaning**: Crazy, wild, insane. It is *fou* reversed. *Un truc de ouf* means a crazy thing.

**Example**: "C'était un concert de ouf." ("That was an insane concert.")

### 7. Relou

**Meaning**: Annoying, a pain. It is *lourd* reversed and works for people and situations.

**Example**: "Il est vraiment relou aujourd'hui." ("He's really annoying today.")

### 8. Chelou

**Meaning**: Shady, weird, dodgy. It is *louche* reversed.

**Example**: "Ce plan est un peu chelou." ("This plan is a bit shady.")

### 9. Vénère

**Meaning**: Angry, worked up. It is *énervé* reversed.

**Example**: "Elle est vénère à cause du retard." ("She's angry because of the delay.")

## French Slang for Reactions

The little words French speakers drop to react without thinking.

### 10. Bof

**Meaning**: Meh. A sound of indifference or being unimpressed.

**Example**: "Le film? Bof, pas terrible." ("The movie? Meh, not great.")

### 11. Grave

**Meaning**: Totally, seriously, for sure. On its own it is strong agreement.

**Example**: "C'était génial, non? Grave!" ("It was great, right? Totally!")

### 12. Nickel

**Meaning**: Perfect, spotless, all set. Handy for confirming a plan.

**Example**: "On se voit à midi? Nickel." ("See you at noon? Perfect.")

### 13. En avoir ras-le-bol

**Meaning**: To be fed up, to have had enough.

**Example**: "J'en ai ras-le-bol de ce boulot." ("I'm fed up with this job.")

## French Slang for Everyday Life

The words that fill ordinary French conversation.

### 14. Kiffer

**Meaning**: To love, to really enjoy something or someone.

**Example**: "Je kiffe cette chanson." ("I love this song.")

### 15. Bouffer

**Meaning**: To eat, to scoff. *La bouffe* is food or grub.

**Example**: "On va bouffer un truc?" ("Shall we go grab a bite?")

### 16. Bosser

**Meaning**: To work, to graft. *Le boulot* is the job or work.

**Example**: "Je dois bosser ce week-end." ("I have to work this weekend.")

### 17. La thune

**Meaning**: Money, cash. You will also hear *le fric* and *le pognon*.

**Example**: "Je n'ai plus de thune." ("I'm out of cash.")

### 18. La bagnole

**Meaning**: Car, motor, ride. A very common informal word for a car.

**Example**: "Sa bagnole est en panne." ("His car has broken down.")

### 19. Les fringues

**Meaning**: Clothes, gear, threads.

**Example**: "J'adore tes fringues." ("I love your clothes.")

### 20. Un truc

**Meaning**: A thing, a whatsit, a thingy. The all purpose filler for anything you cannot name.

**Example**: "Passe-moi ce truc, là." ("Pass me that thing over there.")
